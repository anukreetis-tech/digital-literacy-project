## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where scammers trick users into making unauthorized digital payments or revealing sensitive banking information. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds are becoming increasingly common.

Typically, UPI fraud happens through methods such as fake payment requests, QR code scams, phishing links, or impersonation of bank officials. Fraudsters often create urgency or confusion to manipulate victims into approving transactions or sharing their UPI PIN or OTP. 

A real incident reported in Delhi, March, 2025, involved a victim whose mobile phone was stolen. After the theft, fraudsters reset the device and gained access to the victim’s banking and UPI applications. Using Aadhaar-linked authentication and account recovery methods, they bypassed security features and initiated multiple unauthorized transactions. Within a short period, the victim lost nearly ₹20 lakh before noticing suspicious activity and reporting it to the police. The investigation later led to the arrest of the culprits involved in multiple similar fraud cases. 

How it Happens (Step-by-Step):

The Hook: A fraudster contacts the victim via a marketplace (like OLX) or social media, pretending to be a buyer or a contest organizer.

The Deception: The attacker claims they want to send money to the victim. Instead of sending a "Pay" request, they send a "Collect" (Request Money) link via a UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe).

The Trap: The attacker tells the victim, "Just enter your UPI PIN to receive the payment."

The Theft: Since a UPI PIN is only required to send money, the moment the victim enters their PIN, the amount is instantly debited from their account and transferred to the attacker.

These scams usually target individuals who are less aware of digital security practices, including students, elderly people, and new users of online payments.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty in recovering the stolen money. In many cases, recovery becomes challenging if the fraud is not reported immediately, highlighting the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems.
